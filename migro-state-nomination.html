190 vs 491 — key differences

Both visas award bonus points for state nomination, but have different conditions and pathways to permanent residence.

190
Skilled Nominated
Visa typePermanent
Points bonus+5 pts
Work/live requirementMust live & work in nominating state for 2 years
Occupation listsMLTSSL or STSOL
Who nominatesState/territory govt
PR pathwayImmediate PR on grant
491
Skilled Work Regional (Provisional)
Visa typeProvisional 5 yr
Points bonus+15 pts
Work/live requirementMust live & work in designated regional area
Occupation listsMLTSSL, STSOL, ROL
Who nominatesState govt or eligible relative
PR pathwayApply for 191 after 3 years
📌
Key rule: When granted a 190 or 491, you must initially enter Australia via the nominating state or territory. After that, the residence obligation applies for 2 years (190) or for the duration of the visa (491).

Common nomination requirements

Most states share these core criteria — but each adds its own conditions.

📋
Eligible occupation
Your nominated occupation must be on the state's current occupation list. Lists differ between states — an occupation open in SA may be closed in NSW.
✅
Positive skills assessment
A positive assessment from the relevant assessing authority is required before nomination. Must match your nominated occupation.
🎯
Minimum points score
The federal minimum is 65 points. States may set higher thresholds. Some require 70–75+ for competitive occupations.
🗣️
English proficiency
Minimum Competent English (6.0 each band). ACT and some VIC occupations require Proficient (7.0) or higher.
🏡
Genuine intention to reside
States require a credible commitment to live and work in the state. Evidence: current residence, job offer, family, or property in the state.
💼
Work experience / qualifications
Most states require relevant work experience (typically 3 years in the last 5) or Australian qualifications in the nominated occupation.

Which occupation list do you need?

Your occupation must appear on the correct list for each visa subclass.

List Full name 189 190 491 Description
MLTSSL Medium & Long-term Strategic Skills List ✓ ✓ ✓ High-demand occupations needed long-term. Broadest access.
STSOL Short-term Skilled Occupation List ✗ ✓ ✓ Occupations needed but not long-term priority. Cannot use for 189.
ROL Regional Occupation List ✗ ✗ ✓ Only for 491 regional visa. Occupations needed in regional areas.
💡
Occupation lists are updated regularly. Check the official list before submitting your EOI. Your occupation may move between lists.

How to choose the right state

State nomination is competitive. These strategies improve your chances.

1
Apply to multiple states simultaneously
You can apply for nomination from multiple states at the same time. It's common strategy. If multiple states nominate you, you choose which to use.
2
Match your occupation to state demand
Different states have different workforce needs. Mining suits WA/QLD; policy roles suit ACT; healthcare is in demand everywhere. Research which states prioritise your occupation.
3
Consider smaller states for less competition
NSW and VIC are most competitive. SA, TAS, and NT often have fewer applicants for the same spots — your application may be assessed more favourably with the same score.
4
Build a genuine connection before applying
States take genuine intention seriously. Spend time in your target state before applying. A job offer there is the strongest connection you can demonstrate.
5
491 → 191 pathway: consider the long game
The 491 (+15 pts) is much easier to get invited than 190 (+5 pts). After 3 years in a regional area, apply for the 191 PR. For many, 491 → 191 is faster to PR than waiting for 189.

Common questions

Things people often ask about state nomination.

❓Do I have to stay in the nominating state forever?
▾

No. For 190 visa: 2 years in the nominating state, then you're free to move anywhere. For 491 visa: obligation lasts for the 5-year visa duration. After the 191 PR, no restrictions. The obligation is on a 'best endeavours' basis — genuine emergencies are understood.

❓Can a state reject my nomination application?
▾

Yes. States can decline based on: occupation not on their list, quota filled, insufficient connection, weak evidence of intention to reside, or insufficient points. A rejection from one state doesn't affect others. Apply to multiple states for the best chances.

❓Is there a fee to apply for state nomination?
▾

Most states are free, but some charge a small admin fee (AUD $0–$300). This is separate from the federal visa application charge (several thousand AUD) paid after receiving an ITA. Check each state's current fee schedule.

❓How long does state nomination take?
▾

Typically 4–12 weeks. NT and TAS tend to be faster; NSW and VIC may take longer during peak periods. After nomination, you still need to wait for an ITA through SkillSelect rounds. Total time from applying to state to ITA: 6 weeks to 6+ months.

❓What is 'regional Australia' for the 491 visa?
▾

'Regional Australia' broadly includes everything except Sydney, Melbourne, and Brisbane city centres. Perth, Adelaide, Hobart, Darwin, Canberra, and the Gold Coast all qualify as regional for 491. You can live in a major city — just not Sydney, Melbourne, or inner Brisbane.
