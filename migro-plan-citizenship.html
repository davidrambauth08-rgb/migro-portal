🇦🇺 The Requirements at a Glance

To apply for Australian citizenship by conferral, you must have been a permanent resident for at least 4 years before applying. During those 4 years, you must have been present in Australia for at least 1,460 days total, with at least 365 of those days as a permanent resident in the last 12 months. You must also pass the citizenship test and be of good character.

Confirm 'good character' requirements
Required
▼

'Good character' requires disclosing any criminal history. A single minor offence won't automatically disqualify you, but it must be declared. Any sentence of 12+ months imprisonment in the last 10 years will disqualify you. The Department runs their own police checks.

What to declare
📋Any criminal convictions in Australia or overseas
📋Any apprehended violence orders (AVO/DVO) or court orders
📋Any pending charges or investigations
Tip: If in doubt about whether to declare something, always declare it. Non-disclosure is treated far more seriously than the original offence.

▼

The citizenship test is based on the official resource book 'Australian Citizenship: Our Common Bond.' It has 20 questions; you need 75% (15/20). Most people pass with 1–2 weeks of study. The book is free to download.

Key topics covered
⚖️Australian values: democracy, rule of law, equality, freedom
🏛️Government structure: Parliament, Senate, House of Representatives
📜Australian history: First Nations peoples, Federation, significant events
🌏National symbols: anthem, flag, coat of arms

Learn the national anthem — 'Advance Australia Fair'

▼

At the citizenship ceremony, you'll be expected to sing the first verse of the national anthem. It was updated in January 2021. A meaningful moment worth preparing for in advance.

Fun fact: The anthem's first verse was changed in January 2021 — 'one and free' replaced 'young and free' to better honor First Nations peoples' deep connection to the land.

🇦🇺
Phase 5: The Citizenship Ceremony

After your application is approved, you'll receive an invitation to a citizenship ceremony — held by your local council or the Department. This is where you make your pledge of commitment to Australia and officially become an Australian citizen.

The Australian Citizenship Pledge
"From this time forward..." "...I pledge my loyalty to Australia and its people, whose democratic beliefs I share, whose rights and liberties I respect, and whose laws I will uphold and obey."
🎉 After the ceremony: you'll receive your Australian citizenship certificate. Apply for your Australian passport immediately — it's one of the most powerful travel documents in the world.
