Regional Pathway

491 & 191 — The Regional Pathway to PR

Live and work in regional Australia on a 491, then convert to permanent residence via the 191 after 3 years. Faster than the points test for many applicants.

The full regional pathway

STEP 1
Get state/territory nomination
Apply to a state/territory for 491 nomination. Requires points test score and occupation on relevant list.
→
STEP 2
491 visa granted
5-year provisional visa. Live, work, study in a designated regional area. +15 points bonus included.
→
STEP 3 (3 years later)
Apply for 191 PR
After 3 years residing in regional Australia + meeting income threshold, apply for permanent 191 visa.
→
RESULT
Permanent Resident
Full PR, can live anywhere in Australia. Pathway to citizenship after 4 years of residence.

Visa 491 requirements

📋 Invitation to apply
  • Submit Expression of Interest (EOI) in SkillSelect
  • Receive state/territory nomination
  • Receive Invitation to Apply (ITA) from Home Affairs
  • Minimum 65 points (actual invitations much higher)
🧑‍💼 Personal requirements
  • Under 45 years at time of invitation
  • Competent English (IELTS 6.0 each band)
  • Occupation on relevant occupation list
  • Positive skills assessment
  • Health and character requirements
📍 Regional obligation
  • Must live in a designated regional area
  • Must work/study in the regional area
  • Cannot move to metro Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane, Perth, Gold Coast
  • Obligation applies for the 5-year visa duration
🏛️ State nomination
  • Each state/territory has own criteria
  • Some require you to already be in the region
  • Some require a job offer
  • Additional skills assessment may be required
  • Check each state's current criteria

Visa 191 requirements — the PR stage

✅

3 conditions for the 191

To apply for the Subclass 191 (Permanent Residence — Regional) you must meet all three conditions: (1) Have held a 491, 494, or 887 visa for at least 3 years; (2) Have lived in a designated regional area for at least 3 years; (3) Have earned at least $53,900 per year in taxable income for each of those 3 years.

📅
3 years holding a qualifying visaYou must have held the 491, 494 (Skilled Employer Sponsored Regional), or an old 887 visa for at least 3 years. Time on a Bridging Visa does not count.
📍
3 years residing in regional AustraliaYou must have been physically present in a designated regional area for at least 3 years. Brief absences for holidays or work travel are generally acceptable, but you must genuinely be living in the region.
💰
$53,900 income requirement — each yearYou must have earned at least $53,900 per year in taxable income in each of the 3 qualifying years. This is based on your Australian Tax Office (ATO) tax returns. Only taxable income from regional work qualifies — income from overseas or metro areas does not count.
📋
Health, character, and EnglishYou must still meet health, character, and English requirements at the time of the 191 application. English requirements remain the same (Competent English). You'll need updated health examinations and police clearances.

What counts as 'regional Australia'?

📍

Regional = everything except 5 metro areas

For the 491/191, 'regional Australia' means anywhere in Australia except the metropolitan areas of Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane, Perth, and the Gold Coast. This includes regional cities like Adelaide, Canberra, Hobart, Darwin, Wollongong, Newcastle, Cairns, Townsville, Geelong, and hundreds of smaller towns.

Strategy tips

💡
Adelaide is a great optionAdelaide counts as regional for the 491/191 and offers a city lifestyle, good job market, and strong state nomination program (SA). Many skilled migrants have found a great balance in Adelaide.
📊
Track your income carefullyThe $53,900 income threshold is assessed per tax year. Keep all payslips and tax returns. Make sure your employment contracts reflect your regional work.
📍
Document your regional livingKeep evidence of your regional residence: lease agreements, utility bills, vehicle registration, local bank statements, school enrolment (for children). This is critical for the 191 application.
🎓
Consider regional study firstCompleting a degree in a regional area gives you 10 bonus regional points, a 485 bonus year, and makes you more competitive for regional state nomination (491). The study-to-491-to-191 pipeline is very popular.
⚡
491 can be faster than 189 for lower-points applicantsIf your points score is, say, 75–85, you may wait years in the 189 pool. The 491 with +15 bonus could see you invited much sooner — and the 191 PR comes 3 years after the 491 grant.

Secondary applicants (partner, children) on a 491 visa also have regional conditions. They should also be living in the regional area. Regularly commuting between regional and metro areas for work may be acceptable, but a permanent separation where family lives metro and primary holder lives regional can be complex. Seek migration advice for your specific situation.
The 491 is a 5-year visa and cannot currently be renewed or extended. If you haven't met the 191 requirements within 5 years, you would need to apply for another visa. Planning to meet the 191 criteria (income threshold, regional residence) well within the 5-year window is essential.
The income requirement must be met in each of the 3 qualifying years. If your income drops below the threshold in one year (e.g. due to illness, parental leave, or part-time work), that year may not count, and you'll need additional time to accumulate 3 qualifying years. Plan your finances carefully.
