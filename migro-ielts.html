Tips by skill
Listening
▼
›Practice with Australian accents — BBC 6 Minute English, ABC Radio National.
›Read questions before the audio — you get 30 seconds per section.
›Never leave an answer blank — there's no penalty for wrong answers.
›Sections 3 and 4 are harder — allocate more focus to these in practice.
Reading
▼
›Skim first, scan for answers — don't read every word, you'll run out of time.
›True / False / Not Given: 'Not Given' means the text doesn't mention it at all.
›Aim for 20 minutes per passage — 3 passages, 60 minutes total.
›Build vocabulary: The Guardian, The Economist, academic word lists (AWL).
Writing
▼
›The hardest skill to improve — start preparation at least 3 months before your exam.
›Task 1: describe data objectively. Task 2: structured argument — intro, 2 body paragraphs, conclusion.
›Vary your sentence structures and vocabulary — avoid repeating the same words.
›Get feedback from a teacher or AI — self-assessment misses grammar and coherence issues.
Speaking
▼
›Fluency over accuracy — don't pause to search for the 'perfect' word.
›Part 2 long turn: structure around situation → action → result → how you felt.
›Practice with native speakers — iTalki, Cambly, or free language exchange apps.
›Record yourself daily — reveals filler words, pace issues, and pronunciation patterns.
