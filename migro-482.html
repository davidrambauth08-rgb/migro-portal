Subclass 482 · TSS

Employer-sponsored pathway

The Temporary Skill Shortage visa lets employers sponsor skilled workers when they can't find Australians for the role — and is a major stepping stone to permanent residence.

What is the subclass 482?

Australia's main employer-sponsored temporary work visa. Requires a sponsoring employer and an approved occupation.

🏢
Unlike the 189/190/491 skills-based visas, the 482 requires an employer to sponsor you. Your employer must be an approved sponsor, your occupation must be eligible, and your salary must meet the TSMIT (currently AUD $73,150).
🤝
Employer must sponsor
You cannot self-sponsor. Your employer applies to become an approved sponsor first.
📋
Occupation must be eligible
Must be on MLTSSL (medium-term) or STSOL (short-term stream).
💵
TSMIT salary threshold
Must earn at least AUD $73,150/year (2024–25 TSMIT), updated 1 July each year.
🌍
Labour Market Testing
Most sponsors must prove they tried to hire an Australian first before sponsoring overseas workers.

How the 482 process works

Three separate stages — employer, then you, then the Department.

1
Employer becomes an approved sponsor Employer
Your employer lodges a Standard Business Sponsorship (SBS) application. Costs AUD $420, takes 1–4 weeks. Many larger companies are already approved. The employer commits to paying fair wages and meeting all sponsorship obligations.
2
Employer nominates the position Employer
Employer nominates the specific position and you as the proposed worker. Must prove: genuine position, Labour Market Testing completed (advertising for 2+ weeks), salary meets TSMIT, occupation is eligible.
3
You lodge your visa application Applicant
Submit visa application via ImmiAccount. Requirements: passport, skills assessment (medium-term stream), Competent English, 2 years of relevant work experience, health and character checks.
4
Decision & visa grant Department
Processing: 3–6 months (medium-term), 2–4 months (short-term). Once granted, you work for the sponsoring employer in the nominated role only.

Your rights on a 482 visa

482 holders have strong workplace protections. Know them.

→
You must be paid at least the market salary rate — not just TSMIT
Your employer must pay the 'annual market salary rate' for your role and location — potentially much higher than TSMIT. You cannot be paid less than comparable Australian workers in the same position.
→
You can change employers — with a new nomination
You're not locked in permanently. New employer must submit a fresh nomination. There is a 60-day grace period to find a new sponsor after leaving your employer — check with a migration agent to understand your obligations.
→
Your family members can work and study
Secondary applicants (partner, dependent children) can work for any employer and study without restriction. They are not tied to your sponsoring employer.
→
If your employer goes under, you have options
If your sponsor withdraws or closes, you have a 60-day grace period. During this time: find a new sponsor, apply for a bridging visa, or explore other visa options. Act immediately — don't wait.

Frequently asked questions

❓Can my employer make me pay back the sponsorship costs?
▾

No. This is explicitly illegal. An employer cannot require a sponsored worker to pay or reimburse the sponsorship fee, nomination fee, or SAF levy. Report violations to the Fair Work Ombudsman or Home Affairs.

❓Do I need a skills assessment for the 482?
▾

It depends on the stream and occupation. Medium-term stream: skills assessment required for most occupations. Short-term stream: generally not required — employer demonstrates your skills. Licensed professions require registration with a professional body.

❓Can I bring my family on a 482?
▾

Yes. Partner (spouse or de facto) and dependent children can be included as secondary applicants. They can work for any employer and study without restriction. Children must generally be under 23 (or under 18 if not in full-time study). Family members can also be added after your initial grant.

❓What happens to my visa if my employer fires me?
▾

Your visa doesn't expire immediately — you have a 60-day grace period to find a new approved sponsor. During this period you can remain in Australia but cannot work for a new employer until the new nomination is approved. If no new sponsorship secured in 60 days, you must leave or apply for another visa.

❓Can I apply for the 482 while outside Australia?
▾

Yes. The 482 can be applied for from inside or outside Australia. Once granted, you enter Australia to start work. If already in Australia on another visa, you can apply onshore and may receive a bridging visa while the 482 is processed.
