What can you study on the 500?

🏫
School sector
Primary and secondary school programs at CRICOS-registered schools. Family members of skilled migrants often use this for children.
📜
English Language (ELICOS)
English language intensive courses for overseas students. Often taken before a degree program to meet admission English requirements.
🔧
VET / TAFE
Vocational Education and Training — certificates, diplomas, and advanced diplomas in trades, hospitality, IT, business, and more. Can lead to Graduate Work 485.
🎓
Higher Education
Bachelor, Honours, Masters (coursework or research), and Doctoral degrees at Australian universities. Best pathway to the 485 Post-Study Work stream.

🔬
Postgraduate Research
Masters by research and PhDs. Often come with scholarships (APA, university scholarships). PhD graduates get 4 years on the 485.
See 485 guide →
🌐
Foundation programs
Bridging programs that prepare students for university entry. Often used by students whose secondary qualifications are not recognized for direct university entry.

Eligibility requirements

📝
Genuine Temporary Entrant (GTE)You must demonstrate genuine intent to temporarily enter Australia for study and plan to return home afterward. This is assessed by a case officer based on your personal statement, financial situation, and immigration history.
🎓
Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE)You must have a valid CoE from a CRICOS-registered provider before lodging your visa application. Your institution issues this after you accept their offer and pay the initial tuition fee.
🌐
English language proficiencyRequirements vary by institution and course level. Typically: IELTS Academic 5.5–7.0 depending on course. Some universities accept TOEFL, PTE, or Cambridge. English test must usually be taken within 2 years of application.
💰
Financial capacityYou must show you have enough money to cover: 1st year tuition fees + $21,041 living costs + travel costs. For a partner add $7,362, for each child add $3,152. Evidence can include bank statements, scholarship letters, or sponsor letters.
💊
Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC)You must maintain OSHC for the entire duration of your student visa. Your institution may arrange this for you as part of your enrolment, or you can purchase it independently from approved providers like Allianz, Medibank, or BUPA.
🏥
Health and characterYou must meet health requirements (chest X-ray may be required depending on country of origin) and character requirements (police clearance from any country you've lived in for 12+ months).

Work rights on the 500

Period Work limit Notes
During semester / term 48 hrs per fortnight A fortnight is a 14-day period
During official course breaks / holidays Unlimited Must be enrolled in next semester
Partner / spouse 48 hrs / fortnight OR unlimited Unlimited if primary holder in Masters/PhD
For healthcare / aged care workers Unlimited (temporary measure) Check current government advice
⚠️

Don't exceed the work limit

Exceeding the work hour limit is a visa condition breach. Your employer cannot authorize extra hours — it is your responsibility to track and enforce the limit. Multiple breaches can result in visa cancellation.

How to apply

1
Choose your course and institution
Select a CRICOS-registered institution. Consider: course quality, institution ranking, location (metro vs regional), pathway to PR, and graduate employment rates. Regional institutions often offer lower tuition fees and better 485 bonus years.
2
Apply to the institution and get your CoE
Apply directly to the institution (or via an education agent). Once accepted, pay the required fees and receive your Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE). This is required for your visa application.
3
Arrange OSHC
Purchase Overseas Student Health Cover for the full duration of your visa. Providers include Allianz Care Australia, Medibank, NIB, AHM, and BUPA. Typical cost: $600–$900 per year for a single student.
4
Lodge your visa application on ImmiAccount
Create an ImmiAccount and apply for visa 500. Attach your CoE, passport, financial evidence, OSHC, English test results, and GTE statement. Pay the application charge ($710). Most applications are lodged offshore (from home country) but onshore applications are accepted.
5
Complete health and character checks
You may be asked to complete a health examination and provide police clearances. Book through an approved panel physician for the health exam. Depending on your nationality and time spent in other countries, you may need clearances from multiple countries.
6
Receive grant and travel
Processing typically takes 1–4 weeks for straightforward applications. Once granted, you can travel to Australia and begin your studies. Your visa will usually be granted for the duration of your course plus extra time (typically 2 months after course end).
📝

The GTE requirement

The Genuine Temporary Entrant (GTE) requirement is the most commonly misunderstood part of the student visa. A case officer assesses whether you genuinely intend to study and return home. Strong GTE statements address: why you chose Australia, why this specific course and institution, your ties to your home country (family, job, property), and your study and career goals. Having PR as a long-term goal is not disqualifying — many students are transparent about it. What matters is that your study intentions are genuine.

Study-to-PR strategy

🎯
Choose your occupation firstResearch which occupations are on the MLTSSL and STSOL before choosing your course. Your qualification must match the ANZSCO code of the occupation for skills assessment and points.
📍
Consider regional studyStudying in a regional area gives you a 485 bonus year, up to 10 regional points, and access to 491 state nomination. This can be a significant advantage for PR.
⬆️
Go for a higher degreeA Masters degree gives you 2–3 years on the 485 (vs 2 years for a Bachelor) and more points for the degree level itself. A PhD gives 4 years and the highest qualification points.
💼
Work in your field during studiesInternships and part-time work in your field during your studies count as skilled work experience for the points test — and help with your skills assessment reference letters.
📋
Plan your skills assessment earlyStart your skills assessment application close to graduation — they take 8–16 weeks. Having it ready when you apply for the 485 saves months of waiting.
🤑
Look for scholarshipsAustralia Awards, Destination Australia (regional study), university merit scholarships, and research scholarships can significantly reduce the cost of study and improve your financial visa requirements.

Frequently asked questions

Yes, you can transfer courses. However, if you want to transfer within 6 months of commencing your first course at an institution, you generally need a release letter from your current institution. If you change to a shorter course, your visa validity may be affected. Always notify Home Affairs of any changes via ImmiAccount.
Costs vary widely. Bachelor degree: $20,000–$45,000 per year. Masters: $22,000–$50,000 per year. PhD: often partially or fully funded. VET diploma: $8,000–$20,000 per year. Living costs are approximately $21,000–$28,000 per year depending on the city (Sydney and Melbourne being most expensive).
Dependent children included in your student visa can attend school in Australia. However, they are NOT entitled to free government schooling unless you are covered by a bilateral agreement or scholarship scheme. Most international students' children must pay school fees. Government school fees for international students vary by state: typically $4,000–$6,000 per year.
For the 485 Post-Study Work stream, you need to have studied in Australia for at least 2 years. Online study from outside Australia does not count towards this requirement (with limited COVID-period exceptions). You must physically be in Australia for your studies to count.
