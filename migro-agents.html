How to choose a migration agent

A registered migration agent can make the difference between approval and refusal. Learn what to look for, what to avoid, and how much it should cost.

🏅

Only use a MARA-registered agent

In Australia, it is illegal for anyone other than a registered migration agent (MARA) or a lawyer with the right to practice migration law to provide immigration assistance for a fee. MARA agents are listed on the Office of the Migration Agents Registration Authority (OMARA) register. Always verify your agent's registration at mara.gov.au before engaging them. Unregistered agents ('migration consultants') may be operating illegally.

Do you actually need an agent?

When an agent adds real value

  • Complex visa types (186, partner visa, refugee)
  • Previous visa refusal or cancellation
  • Character issues (criminal record, overstay)
  • Unusual employment history or gaps
  • Complex family situation (divorced, children)
  • Employer who is new to sponsorship (482/186)
  • Time-sensitive applications
  • You are not confident in English

When you might be fine DIY

  • Straightforward 189/190 with clean history
  • 485 graduate visa (usually simple)
  • Student visa with clear CoE and finances
  • You have time to research carefully
  • Your occupation, points, and documents are clear-cut
  • You've already had a paid consultation and know your path

Red flags — avoid these agents

🚩
Guarantees visa successNo legitimate agent can guarantee a visa outcome. The decision belongs to the Department of Home Affairs. Anyone who guarantees approval is misleading you.
🚩
Very cheap feesExperienced registered agents charge professional rates. If fees seem too good to be true (e.g. $200 for a partner visa), the agent may be unqualified, outsourcing to unlicensed staff, or cutting corners on your application.
🚩
Won't provide a written contractMARA agents are legally required to provide a written contract before accepting payment. If an agent asks for money without a written agreement, that is a serious red flag.
🚩
Can't be found on MARA registerBefore signing anything, search the OMARA register at mara.gov.au. If the person isn't listed or is listed as 'cancelled' or 'barred', do not engage them — they are not legally allowed to charge you for advice.
🚩
Asks you to misrepresent informationIf an agent suggests you exaggerate qualifications, misrepresent work history, or provide false documents, they are asking you to commit immigration fraud. This can result in visa cancellation and a permanent ban.
🚩
Pressures you to decide immediatelyHigh-pressure sales tactics — 'This offer is only good today' — are a warning sign. A reputable agent will give you time to consider and ask questions.
🚩
'Connections' at Home AffairsNo agent has 'connections' that can influence visa decisions. Any agent who claims special access to decision-makers is lying. Visa decisions are made by case officers applying the law.
🚩
Based overseas with no Australian licenseIf you want to migrate to Australia, your migration agent must be a MARA-registered agent in Australia (or an Australian lawyer). 'Migration consultants' from your home country may not be legally authorised to advise on Australian law.

What to look for in a good agent

✓ Green flags

  • Verifiable MARA registration number
  • Specialises in your visa type
  • Provides a written contract before payment
  • Clear fee structure — no hidden costs
  • Explains the risks and realistic timeline
  • Responsive and communicative
  • Positive reviews from real clients
  • Asks detailed questions about your situation
  • Uses Australian Immigration law firm or MARA office

✗ Red flags

  • Guarantees visa outcome
  • Very cheap or unusually expensive
  • Not on the MARA register
  • No written contract before payment
  • Suggests hiding or misrepresenting information
  • Hard to reach — doesn't return calls/emails
  • Claims 'special contacts' at immigration
  • High-pressure sales, urgency tactics
  • Based overseas, not licensed in Australia

Beware of 'unbundled' services

Some agents quote a low initial fee but charge extra for every communication, document review, or form submission. Ask upfront: 'Is this an all-inclusive fee or will there be additional charges?' Get a detailed scope of work in writing.

Questions to ask a potential agent

"What is your MARA registration number?"
Verify it yourself at mara.gov.au before proceeding.
"How many cases like mine have you handled?"
A specialist in your visa type will be more effective than a generalist.
"What is your all-inclusive fee?"
Get a written quote covering all services, not just initial lodgement.
"What are the realistic risks in my application?"
A good agent will identify weaknesses honestly, not just promise success.
"Who will handle my case day-to-day?"
Ensure it's the registered agent, not unlicensed support staff.
"Do you have references or reviews I can check?"
Check Google, Facebook, or community forums for real client feedback.
"What happens if my application is refused?"
Understand their policy on refusals — do they offer appeals assistance?
"Will you keep me informed at each step?"
Communication standards vary widely — set expectations upfront.
⚖️

If something goes wrong

If you have a complaint about a registered migration agent, you can lodge a complaint with the Office of the Migration Agents Registration Authority (OMARA) at mara.gov.au. If you believe you are a victim of migration fraud (e.g. an unregistered agent took your money), contact the Australian Federal Police or your state police. You may also be able to claim compensation through civil proceedings.
