EOI & SkillSelect

How invitations work

Everything you need to know about submitting an Expression of Interest and getting invited to apply.

What is an EOI?

An Expression of Interest is not a visa application — it's your profile in the SkillSelect pool.

🎯
Expression of Interest
You submit an EOI via ImmiAccount to indicate your interest in a skilled visa. You are NOT yet applying for the visa.
🔄
SkillSelect Pool
Your EOI sits in a pool with thousands of other candidates. The Department ranks everyone by points, then date of EOI submission.
✉️
Invitation to Apply (ITA)
If invited, you receive an ITA and have 60 days to lodge a full visa application with all supporting documents.
⚡
Invitation Rounds
The Department typically runs rounds twice per month. Each round has a cutoff (points score + date) called the Closing Date for Invitations (CDI).

Step-by-step process

From skills assessment to visa grant — what to expect at each stage.

1
Get a positive skills assessment Before EOI
You must have a positive outcome from the relevant assessing authority for your nominated occupation before lodging an EOI. This can take weeks to months.
2
Meet English language requirements Before EOI
Minimum: Competent English (IELTS 6.0 each band). For additional points: Proficient (7.0) or Superior (8.0) English is needed. Test results must be valid (≤3 years).
3
Create ImmiAccount and submit EOI
Log in to ImmiAccount on the Home Affairs website. Complete the EOI form carefully — errors can affect your points score. Select all visa subclasses you're eligible for (189, 190, 491).
4
Wait in the SkillSelect pool
Your EOI remains active for 2 years. You can update it at any time — but if you change your score upward, your 'date in pool' resets for that score. Higher points = higher priority in each round.
5
Receive Invitation to Apply (ITA) 60-day deadline
You'll be notified via ImmiAccount. You have exactly 60 days to lodge your complete visa application. Extensions are not granted — missing this deadline means starting over.
6
Lodge full visa application
Submit all evidence: skills assessment, English results, employment records, health, character checks. Pay the visa application charge. Then wait for a decision (typically 6–18 months).

Quick invitation estimate

Maximise your invitation chances

Strategic moves you can make while waiting in the SkillSelect pool.

1
Submit your EOI as early as possible
When two candidates have the same points score, the one who submitted their EOI earlier gets priority. Don't wait until everything is 'perfect' — submit once you meet minimum requirements and update later.
2
Apply for all visa subclasses you're eligible for
You can nominate for 189, 190, and 491 in a single EOI. State nomination (190) often has lower points cutoffs than 189 — being flexible dramatically increases your chances.
3
Retake English test if you can improve your band
Going from Competent (6.0) to Proficient (7.0) adds 10 points; from Competent to Superior (8.0) adds 20 points. This is often the fastest way to jump from 80 to 90+ points.
4
Accumulate Australian work experience while waiting
If you're already in Australia on a working visa, every year of skilled work in your nominated occupation adds points — up to 20 pts for 8+ years. Working toward the 1, 3, 5, or 8-year thresholds can be game-changing.
5
Check state nomination lists regularly
State nomination (190/491) availability changes throughout the year. States open and close their lists, change occupations, and alter point requirements. Checking monthly can reveal new opportunities.
6
Consider a Professional Year program
If you studied in Australia and are in IT, Engineering, or Accounting, completing a Professional Year program earns 5 bonus points. Combined with other improvements, it can push you to the invitation threshold.

After receiving your ITA

A typical timeline from invitation to visa grant. Times are indicative.

Day 0
Invitation to Apply received
Clock starts. You have 60 days to lodge.
Week 1–3
Gather all documents
Employment references, tax records, skills assessment, English test, police clearances, medical schedule.
Week 3–5
Complete health examinations
Book with an approved panel physician. Usually chest X-ray + general medical; biometrics if required.
Day 45–55
Lodge visa application
Upload all documents in ImmiAccount and pay the visa application charge (VAC). Keep buffer time before the 60-day deadline.
Month 2–12+
Processing & assessment
A case officer is assigned. They may request further information (s56 request). Respond promptly. Processing times vary: 189 can be 6–12 months; 190 varies by state.
Finalised
Visa granted 🎉
You'll receive notification via ImmiAccount and email. For 190/491, you typically must travel to and first enter through the nominating state.

Frequently asked questions

Common questions about the EOI and SkillSelect system.

❓Can I update my EOI after submitting it?
▾

Yes — you can update your EOI at any time while it's active. However, be aware of the tie-breaker rule: if you increase your points score, your 'time in pool' for that score resets to the date of the update. This means you go back to the 'end of the queue' for candidates at that new score.

❓How long is an EOI valid?
▾

An EOI is valid for 2 years from the date it was submitted. If you haven't been invited within 2 years, you'll need to submit a new EOI. Note that when you resubmit, your 'date in pool' starts fresh.

❓What is a CDI (Closing Date for Invitations)?
▾

The CDI is the tie-breaker date used when multiple candidates have the same points score. When the Department runs a round, they invite candidates in order of points. If they reach a score where more candidates exist than spots available, they use the CDI date as a tie-breaker.

❓Can I be invited for multiple visas at once?
▾

You can have EOIs for 189, 190, and 491 simultaneously and could receive invitations for multiple visas. However, you can only lodge one visa application at a time — so you'd need to choose. You cannot hold two active visa applications.

❓What happens if I decline an invitation?
▾

If you decline an ITA or let it expire, your EOI remains in the SkillSelect pool and you can be invited again in future rounds. Your 'date in pool' is NOT reset by declining. However, for 190 visas, states may note your decline.

❓Do I need a migration agent to submit an EOI?
▾

No — you can submit an EOI yourself through ImmiAccount for free. However, many people use a Registered Migration Agent (RMA) to ensure accuracy. Errors in your EOI can result in your application being refused after invitation — accuracy is crucial.

  • MARA register (official agent lookup): www.mara.gov.au
  • EOI submission is free; visa lodgement fees are set by the Department.
❓My occupation isn't on the MLTSSL. Can I still get a 189?
▾

The MLTSSL is required for the subclass 189 visa. If your occupation is only on the STSOL, you may still be eligible for state-nominated visas (190/491) depending on the state, but not for 189. Check current lists on the Home Affairs website — they're updated periodically.
