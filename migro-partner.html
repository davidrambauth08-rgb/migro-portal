Partner Visas — 820/801 & 309/100

Bring your partner to Australia as a permanent resident. The partner visa pathway is a two-stage process — a temporary visa first, then permanent residence.

One lodgement, two visas

You only lodge once and pay once. When you lodge the 820/309 application, you are simultaneously applying for the permanent stage (801/100) too. You don't need to re-apply — the department automatically assesses Stage 2 eligibility after 2 years.

Sponsor eligibility

👤

Who can be a sponsor?

To sponsor a partner, you must be: an Australian citizen, Australian permanent resident, or eligible New Zealand citizen. You must be at least 18 years old. You must not have previously been refused as a sponsor or had a sponsorship cancelled. There are restrictions if you have prior sponsorship history — in general, you can only sponsor 2 partners in a lifetime, and must wait 5 years between sponsorships (unless the previous relationship ended due to family violence or death).

De facto relationship requirements

What counts as a de facto relationship?

A de facto relationship is a genuine relationship between two adults living together on a genuine domestic basis. For a partner visa, you need to demonstrate you have been in a de facto relationship for at least 12 months before applying. This 12-month rule can be waived if:

  • You are registered as de facto in an Australian state/territory
  • You have a child together
  • There are compelling and compassionate circumstances

Note: Marriage does not require a 12-month waiting period — you can apply immediately after marrying.

Relationship evidence

You must provide evidence across 4 categories. Strong applications include multiple pieces of evidence in each category.

🏠 Financial aspects
  • Joint bank account statements
  • Joint mortgage / lease agreements
  • Joint utility bills
  • Insurance naming partner as beneficiary
  • Joint assets (car registration, property)
🏡 Nature of household
  • Lease/mortgage in both names
  • Proof of shared address (same mail)
  • Statutory declaration on living together
  • Domestic responsibilities arrangement
👨‍👩‍👧 Social aspects
  • Photos together over time
  • Joint travel — passport stamps / bookings
  • Letters from family / friends
  • Invitations addressed to both
  • Social media presence together
💞 Commitment
  • Marriage certificate (if married)
  • Knowledge of each other's family / background
  • Future plans (statutory declarations)
  • Nomination as next of kin / emergency contact
  • Wills naming each other

Two-stage timeline

1

Lodge application (820 or 309)

Pay the VAC ($9,095 primary), submit all evidence, and lodge on ImmiAccount. From this date, the 2-year clock begins. Stage 1 grant is usually 12–36 months. You receive a bridging visa while waiting.

2

Stage 1 granted (820 or 309)

Full work rights, Medicare access (820), travel in/out of Australia. You can live and work as normal. If you haven't been together 2 years from the application date, you simply wait on this temporary visa.

3

Stage 2 — Permanent visa (801 or 100)

After 2 years together from the original application date, the department assesses Stage 2. You must prove the relationship is still genuine and ongoing. If all is in order, the permanent visa (801 or 100) is granted — usually 24–36 months from original application.

Strategy tips

📁
Document everything from the startStart building your relationship evidence from day one. Keep joint bank statements, photos with dates, travel records. The more evidence the better — case officers look for depth and consistency.
💌
Get strong statutory declarationsThird-party statutory declarations from people who know you as a couple are very powerful evidence. Get declarations from family and friends who can describe the relationship in detail.
⏰
Apply as soon as eligibleThe 2-year clock for Stage 2 starts from your application date, not when Stage 1 is granted. Applying earlier means the permanent visa comes sooner — even if Stage 1 is still processing.
🛡️
Know your rights if the relationship endsIf family violence occurs, the applicant may still be eligible for the permanent visa even if the relationship ends. Australia takes family violence in partner visa situations very seriously.
🤝
Consider a migration agentPartner visas are complex and expensive. A small mistake can cause delays or refusals. A registered migration agent can review your evidence, identify gaps, and ensure your application is as strong as possible.

Frequently asked questions

For the offshore 309 pathway, you must wait until the 309 is granted before entering Australia and working.
Yes. Dependent children can be included in your partner visa application. Each additional applicant over 18 pays an additional $4,550 and those under 18 pay $2,275. Children included in the application will also receive permanent residence when you do (Stage 2).
For a standard partner visa, you need to have met your partner in person at least once (the 'met in person' requirement). If you haven't met, you may need to consider a Prospective Marriage (Fiancé) Visa (subclass 300) instead, which allows you to come to Australia to marry. After marrying, you can then apply for the partner visa.
If the relationship ends before the Stage 2 permanent visa is assessed, the application is generally refused. However, there are exceptions: if the relationship ended due to the death of the sponsor, or if family violence was involved (the applicant may still be granted PR under the domestic violence provisions). If you have dependent children of the relationship, there may also be pathways.
Only Australian citizens and permanent residents can sponsor a partner for a partner visa. A 482 temporary visa holder cannot directly sponsor a partner for the partner visa. The partner would need to apply for a different visa (e.g. a secondary 482 attached to the primary 482 holder, or the partner could be included in the primary 482 application as a secondary applicant from the beginning).
