Subclass 186 — Employer Nomination Scheme

Permanent residence through employer sponsorship. One of the clearest paths to Australian PR for skilled workers already in Australia or offshore.

How the 186 works

1
Employer becomes an approved sponsor
Your employer must apply to become a Standard Business Sponsor (SBS). Most legitimate employers qualify. This step costs $420 and takes 1–4 weeks. If your employer already sponsors 482 workers, they are likely already an SBS.
2
Employer nominates you
The employer lodges a nomination application for the position. They must show the role is full-time, permanent, and that you meet the requirements. Your occupation must be on the MLTSSL. Nomination fee: $330.
3
Get skills assessed (Direct Entry)
If applying via Direct Entry stream, you need a positive skills assessment from the relevant assessing authority for your occupation (e.g. ACS for IT, Engineers Australia for engineering). Transition stream applicants are usually exempt.
4
Lodge your visa application
Once nominated, you can lodge the visa application on ImmiAccount. Include health examinations, police clearances, English evidence, and skills assessment. You can lodge nomination and visa concurrently.
5
Await decision
Processing times range from 5–18 months. You'll receive a bridging visa if you're onshore and your current visa expires. Once granted, you receive permanent residence immediately.

Core requirements

🧑‍💼 Applicant — Transition Stream
  • Hold or have held a 482 or 457 visa
  • 3 years with same employer in same role
  • Under 45 at time of application
  • Competent English (IELTS 6.0 each band)
  • Occupation on MLTSSL
  • Pass health and character checks
🌏 Applicant — Direct Entry Stream
  • 3 years relevant work experience
  • Positive skills assessment
  • Under 45 at time of application
  • Competent English (IELTS 6.0 each band)
  • Occupation on MLTSSL
  • Pass health and character checks
🏢 Employer requirements
  • Approved Standard Business Sponsor
  • Genuine need for the position
  • Training obligations met (2% levy)
  • Salary meets TSMIT ($73,150)
  • Equivalent terms to Australian workers
  • No adverse information / sanctions
🌐 English language
  • IELTS: 6.0 each band (Competent)
  • OET: B in all four components
  • TOEFL iBT: 12L, 13R, 18S, 21W
  • PTE: 50 each component
  • Passport holders from UK, USA, CA, NZ, IE exempt
⚠️

Age limit: under 45

You must be under 45 years of age at the time you lodge your visa application (not nomination). Certain academic and medical professionals may be exempt — check with a registered migration agent. Employers cannot apply for an age waiver unless you fall under specific exemption categories.

⚠️

SAF levy is the employer's cost

It is illegal for an employer to recover the SAF levy or nomination costs from the worker. If an employer asks you to pay these, that is a red flag and may constitute visa fraud.

Strategy tips

🎯
Start the conversation earlyDon't wait until your 482 expires. Raise the 186 pathway with your employer well before the 3-year mark — processing can take over a year.
📋
Keep clean employment recordsFor the Transition Stream, you need to prove 3 continuous years. Keep payslips, tax returns, and employment contracts. Any gaps can be problematic.
🌏
Onshore vs offshore mattersIf you apply onshore, you'll get a Bridging Visa A while waiting. If offshore, you must wait abroad. Onshore is generally preferred.
📝
MLTSSL check firstUnlike the 482, the 186 requires your occupation to be on the MLTSSL. Check before starting the process — some occupations on the STSOL don't qualify.
⏰
Watch your ageIf you're approaching 45, timing is critical. The age is assessed at visa application, not nomination. Start early if you're getting close.
🤝
Employer educationMany employers don't know the 186 process. Be prepared to explain the costs and steps to HR or management. A good migration agent can help with employer communication.

Frequently asked questions

For the Transition Stream, changing employers would disrupt your 3-year continuity requirement and could cause your nomination to be withdrawn. For the Direct Entry stream, a change of employer requires a new nomination but you can transfer the visa application to the new employer's nomination. This is a complex situation — consult a migration agent.
For the 186 (permanent visa), the SAF levy is paid as a one-time payment at nomination, not annually. Small businesses (turnover under $10M) pay $3,000 and larger businesses pay $5,000 per nominated position.
Most skills assessments are valid for 3 years from the date of the assessment (not the date of your visa application). If yours is expiring or has expired, you may need to apply for a reassessment. Some bodies like ACS offer extensions or re-assessments at reduced cost.
Yes. Your partner and dependent children can be included in your 186 visa application. They will also receive permanent residence and will be able to work and study in Australia. Each additional applicant incurs their own VAC.
The 186 is a permanent visa — you're an Australian permanent resident from day one. You can live, work, and study anywhere in Australia, sponsor eligible relatives, and after 4 years of residence, apply for Australian citizenship. You're not tied to the original employer after the visa is granted.
