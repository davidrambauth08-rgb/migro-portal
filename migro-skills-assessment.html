Why do you need a skills assessment?

It's the first mandatory step before submitting an EOI for any skilled visa.

🎯
A skills assessment is an official evaluation confirming your qualifications and work experience are comparable to Australian standards. Without a positive outcome, you cannot submit an EOI or be invited to apply for a skilled visa.
⏱️
Takes weeks to months
Plan ahead — don't wait until you're ready to apply.
📌
Occupation-specific
Each occupation has one designated assessing authority — you can't choose.
🔄
Valid for 3 years
Most assessments are valid for 3 years from the assessment letter date.
💸
Paid service
Costs range from AUD $300 to $1,000+ depending on authority.

Documents you'll typically need

Requirements vary by authority, but these core documents are almost always required.

🎓
Academic transcripts & degree certificates
Official transcripts with subjects and grades. Degree certificate showing conferral. Certified copies usually required. Non-English documents need NAATI-certified translation.
RequiredGet certified copies
💼
Employment reference letters
On company letterhead, signed by manager/HR. Must include: dates, full-time/part-time hours, job title, and detailed duties description. Vague letters are a top reason for negative assessments.
RequiredDetail duties specifically
📄
Payslips / payroll records
Multiple payslips per period to corroborate employment dates. Bank statements or tax returns can substitute if payslips are unavailable.
Required
🪪
Identity documents
Passport including all pages showing travel history. Some authorities also require birth certificate. Must be certified copies.
Required
🌐
Certified translations (if applicable)
Any non-English document must be translated by a NAATI-accredited translator. The translation must accompany the original document.
If not in EnglishNAATI only

Statutory declaration or CV (sometimes)
Some authorities (e.g. VETASSESS) require a statutory declaration detailing duties. Particularly important when reference letters are insufficient. A detailed CV is almost always useful to include.

Tips for a strong assessment

The most common reasons for negative outcomes — and how to avoid them.

1
Get specific, detailed employment reference letters
The #1 reason for negative assessments is vague reference letters. Draft the letter yourself detailing: daily tasks, technologies/tools used, projects led, and how your role matches the nominated occupation. Ask the manager to review and sign it on letterhead.
2
Match your duties to the ANZSCO description
Authorities compare your duties against the ANZSCO description. Read it before writing reference letters — use the same language and cover all key tasks. Gaps between actual duties and ANZSCO description will hurt your outcome.
3
Don't round up your experience period
Inconsistencies between claimed dates and supporting documents (payslips, contracts, LinkedIn) raise credibility concerns. Be exact with all dates. You can reapply for reassessment once you have more experience.
4
Apply as early as possible in your preparation
Processing takes 4–24 weeks. Apply early so delays don't hold up your EOI. You can submit an EOI before the assessment is final — update it once you get the outcome. Most applicants regret waiting too long.
5
If refused, you can request a review or reapply
A negative assessment is not the end. Most authorities offer a review process. You can also reapply with stronger evidence. Common fixes: more detailed reference letters, a statutory declaration, or clarifying documentation.

Common questions

❓Can I change my nominated occupation after getting assessed?
▾

You can change your nominated occupation in your EOI, but if the new occupation has a different assessing authority, you'll need a new assessment. Some authorities allow adding occupations for a smaller fee. Check each authority's policy.

❓Does my assessment count the same points if I have only overseas experience?
▾

The skills assessment produces a positive/negative outcome only. Points for work experience are calculated separately in your EOI. Australian experience is worth more points than overseas experience per year. The authority confirms your experience is genuine; Home Affairs assigns points.

❓What if my employer is no longer in business?
▾

Authorities are familiar with this. Alternative evidence: bank statements showing salary deposits, tax returns, statutory declarations from former colleagues, LinkedIn history, superannuation records, project documentation. A statutory declaration explaining the situation with alternative evidence is usually accepted.

❓Can I get fast-tracked processing?
▾

Some authorities offer priority processing for an extra fee. ACS offers ~2–4 week priority service. VETASSESS also offers priority assessment. Engineers Australia and TRA do not have formal priority services. Even 'fast' processing typically takes 2–6 weeks.
